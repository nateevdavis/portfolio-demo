Overview

Aesthete is a personal project. While I was enrolled in the University of Texas UI/UX Bootcamp I led a group project built around my idea of creating a platform for small businesses to find local artists and rent their art. I enjoyed the project, and decided to revisit and redesign the app.

Mission Statement

Aesthete is a mobile first website designed to connect local artists and businesses in order to foster a symbiotic relationship that encourages continued investment and promotion of the arts.

The Problem

Local businesses need to decorate their space.

Local artists need more opportunities to showcase their work, as well as more regular streams of income.

Aesthete bridges the gap, providing a marketplace for businesses to find unique art made by creators in their area.

High Level Goals

Goal

- Search for artists by location and style
- Check how a piece would look in their building

Solution

- Create searchable map layers that can be filtered by art styles
- Create a “see it in your space” feature, where businesses can use AR to see a piece on their wall

Trends and Market Research

Main Compeitors

Rise Art: UK based company that acts as a curator who sources artists, and provides art to clients based on an art personality quiz
Turning Art: Art rental company that acts as a curation middle-man, where experts work closely with businesses to provide art and rotate it
Hang Art: San Fransisco based art rental company, but it selects which artists it features

Design Inspiration

Airbnb: The flexibility Airbnb offers to users, coupled with the simplicity of their flows, breaks an otherwise daunting task down to a few easy steps.
Facebook Marketplace: Facebook Marketplace connects buyers and sellers in an easy way without the need for administrative intervention.
